Nobel licensed his invention to the San Francisco merchant Julius Bandmann and in
1867 the first explosives manufacturing firm in the United States known as the Giant
Powder Company was established in the East Bay. Due to continual outcry about the
environmental devastation they caused the factory relocated several times before
being constructed on Fleming Point within what is now known as the Albany Bulb.

Workers at the plant accidentally ignited nitroglycerin on the morning of July 9th, 1892 -
setting off a series of seven explosions that killed everyone on site instantly. Because
most employees at Giant Powder were Chinese immigrants, they were singled out in
the press as who to blame for the destruction of both the shoreline and the windows
of the UC Berkeley campus which all shattered from the force of the explosions.
